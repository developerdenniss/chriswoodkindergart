Baby Care

How to pay attention to your child?

Active Listening: Give your child your full attention when they speak. Avoid distractions (like phones or TV) and make eye contact. Acknowledge their feelings with simple responses, like "I understand." Quality Time: Spend uninterrupted time with your child doing activities they enjoy (playing, reading, crafting). Create moments for connection, like family dinners or weekend outings. Be Present: Focus on your child's needs in the moment, whether it's helping with homework or comforting them when upset. Show interest in their day, thoughts, and experiences. Encourage Open Communication: Foster a safe space where your child feels comfortable expressing themselves. Ask open-ended questions to promote conversation, like "What was the best part of your day?" Physical Touch: Offer affection through hugs, high-fives, or gentle pats to show you care. Physical connection strengthens emotional bonds. Observe Non-Verbal Cues: Watch for signs of emotions or needs that aren’t directly spoken, such as body language or facial expressions.
